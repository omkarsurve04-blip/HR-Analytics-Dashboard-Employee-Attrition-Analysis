# HR-Analytics-Dashboard-Employees-Attrition-Analysis

## Project Overview

**Project Title**: HR Analytics Dashboard Employee Attrition Analysis 
**Level**: Beginner  

This project analyzes employee attrition patterns to identify why employees leave and which workforce segments are most at risk.

## Objectives

Using Power BI and Excel/SQL, I built an interactive HR dashboard that tracks key KPIs, employee demographics, salary trends, and department-level attrition to support data-driven HR decisions.

The goal is to help organizations:

- Reduce employee turnover

- Improve retention strategies

- Optimize workforce planning

## Insights Discovered

## Attrition Trends 

- Highest attrition in 26–35 age group
- Male attrition > Female attrition
- Majority exits from ≤ 5K salary band

## By Job Role

- Laboratory Technician → 62
- Sales Executive → 57
- Research Scientist → 47
- Sales Representative → 33

## By Education

- Life Sciences & Medical backgrounds show higher churn

## By Tenure

- Most employees leave within first few years of joining
